## What is Pramukh Men's Wear?

Pramukh Men's Wear is a comprehensive retail management system designed for multi-branch clothing stores. It provides a complete solution for inventory management, point-of-sale operations, customer relationship management, and business analytics.

Built with modern technologies including Next.js App Router, Supabase for real-time database operations, and a beautiful Tailwind-based UI, the system offers a seamless experience for retail operations across multiple branches.

## Core modules

### Dashboard

The dashboard provides an at-a-glance view of your retail operations:

- **Sales metrics**: Total sales count and revenue by branch
- **Outstanding dues**: Track pending payments from credit customers
- **Customer base**: Total unique customers across the system
- **Purchase spend**: Inventory investment from vendors
- **Branch assignments**: Switch between branches with role-based access
- **Quick actions**: Jump to billing, inventory, or reporting workflows

### Inventory system

Comprehensive inventory management includes:

- **Categories**: Organize products by type (shirts, trousers, accessories) with size type configuration (alpha, numeric, free size)
- **Items**: Product master data with codes, names, and descriptions
- **Variants**: SKU-level tracking with size, color, base cost, and selling price
- **Colors**: Maintain color catalog with hex values for visual reference
- **Stock tracking**: Real-time stock levels updated via purchases and sales

### Sales & POS

Streamlined point-of-sale operations:

- **Quick billing**: Fast invoice generation with automatic calculations
- **Payment methods**: Support for cash, UPI, card, and other payment types
- **Partial payments**: Track paid amounts and outstanding dues per sale
- **Customer association**: Link sales to customer profiles for history tracking
- **Sale items**: Line-item detail with quantity, price, and discount support
- **Recent sales view**: Live table of latest transactions with status

### Purchases

Vendor and procurement management:

- **Purchase orders**: Record incoming inventory with invoice tracking
- **Vendor management**: Maintain supplier database with contact details and GST numbers
- **Purchase items**: Line-item tracking with quantity, cost, and discounts
- **Vendor payments**: Payment history with method and reference tracking
- **Branch allocation**: Assign purchases to specific branch locations

### Customer management

Build customer relationships:

- **Customer profiles**: Full name, phone, email, and notes
- **Purchase history**: View all sales linked to each customer
- **Payment tracking**: Monitor outstanding dues and payment schedules
- **Customer payments**: Record payments against specific sales or general account credits

### Returns & exchanges

Handle post-sale transactions:

- **Return transactions**: Process product returns with refund amounts
- **Exchanges**: Swap returned items for new products
- **Return reasons**: Track why products are returned for quality insights
- **Inventory adjustments**: Automatically update stock on returns/exchanges
- **Transaction linking**: Connect returns to original sale records

## User roles & permissions

The system supports three role levels per branch:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Owner">
    Full system access including branch creation, user management, and all operational features. Can manage configurations and view cross-branch reports.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Manager">
    Branch-level management with access to inventory, purchases, sales reports, and customer management. Cannot modify system configurations or create branches.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Cashier">
    Point-of-sale operations only. Can process sales, accept payments, and view recent transactions. Limited access to reports and no inventory modification rights.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Users can be assigned different roles across different branches. For example, a user might be an Owner at the main showroom but a Manager at a satellite location.
</Warning>

## Database architecture

The system uses a relational PostgreSQL database with the following key tables:

```sql
-- Core entities
branch              -- Retail locations
user_profile        -- User accounts linked to Supabase auth
branch_user         -- User-to-branch role assignments

-- Inventory
category            -- Product categories with size types
color               -- Color catalog
item                -- Product master
item_variant        -- SKU-level with size, color, pricing

-- Purchasing
vendor              -- Supplier database
purchase            -- Purchase orders
purchase_item       -- Purchase line items
vendor_payment      -- Vendor payment tracking

-- Sales
customer            -- Customer profiles
sale                -- Sales invoices
sale_item           -- Sale line items
customer_payment    -- Customer payment tracking

-- Returns
return_transaction  -- Return/exchange header
return_item         -- Returned items
exchange_item       -- Exchanged items
```

All tables include audit timestamps (`created_at`, `updated_at`) with automatic triggers for consistency.
