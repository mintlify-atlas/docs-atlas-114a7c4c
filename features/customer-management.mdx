The Customer Management system maintains detailed customer profiles, tracks spending patterns, and monitors outstanding dues across all transactions.

## Overview

Customer records are organization-wide (shared across branches) but sales data is filtered by your active branch. The system provides search, pagination, and detailed analytics for customer relationship management.

<Note>
You must be assigned to a branch before managing customers. Without branch assignment, you'll see "No branch assigned" with instructions to contact your administrator.
</Note>

## Search Functionality

The search feature supports filtering customers by:

- **Full name** (case-insensitive partial match)
- **Phone number** (case-insensitive partial match)

Search uses the ILIKE SQL operator with wildcard matching, so searching "John" will find "John Smith", "Johnny Doe", etc.

<Tip>
Search results are case-insensitive and support partial matches, making it easy to find customers even if you only remember part of their name or phone number.
</Tip>

## Customer Table

The Top Customers table displays up to 12 customers sorted by total spend in descending order:

### Columns

- **Name** - Customer's full name in medium font weight
- **Phone** - Phone number, or "—" if not provided
- **Total Spend** - Lifetime revenue from this customer in Indian Rupees
- **Outstanding** - Current unpaid balance, or "—" if fully paid
- **Last Visit** - Date of most recent sale in "DD MMM YYYY" format, or "Never" if no sales

### Empty State

When no customers are linked to sales:

> "No customers linked to sales yet. Complete a sale with a saved customer to see insights here."

## Pagination

The customer directory displays 10 customers per page with full pagination controls:

- **Previous** button (disabled on first page)
- **Page numbers** (with ellipsis for large page counts)
- **Next** button (disabled on last page)

Pagination range calculation:

- Always shows first and last page numbers
- Shows current page ±1 page
- Uses ellipsis when gaps exist
- Maximum of 7 visible page indicators

<Note>
Pagination state is preserved when searching - your search query carries through page navigation.
</Note>

## Adding Customers

Click the add customer button to open the **Add Customer Dialog** where you can enter:

- Full name (required)

## Customer Analytics

For each customer, the system automatically calculates:

### Financial Metrics

- **Total Spend** - Sum of all `total_amount` from sales
- **Paid** - Sum of all `paid_amount` collected
- **Due** - Sum of all `due_amount` still outstanding

### Activity Tracking

- **Last Sale Date** - Most recent `sale_date` from transactions
- **Sales History** - All transactions linked to the customer

## Best Practices

### Data Collection

1. **Capture Phone Numbers** - Always collect phone numbers for SMS communication and search efficiency
2. **Verify Before Saving** - Double-check phone numbers and names for accuracy
3. **Use Consistent Formatting** - Enter names in proper title case (John Smith, not JOHN SMITH)
4. **Add Notes Proactively** - Document customer preferences during the sale

### Outstanding Management

1. **Daily Review** - Check the Page Outstanding metric each morning
2. **Sort by Dues** - Prioritize customers with highest outstanding balances
3. **Follow Up Promptly** - Contact customers with pending payments within 3-5 days
4. **Record Payments Immediately** - Update payment records as soon as dues are collected

### Search Efficiency

1. **Use Partial Names** - Search with first name only if unsure of full name
2. **Try Phone Search** - Phone numbers are often easier to remember than names
3. **Check Spelling** - If search fails, try alternate spellings
4. **Reset Filters** - Use the Reset button to clear search and see all customers

### Loyalty Building

1. **Track Total Spend** - Identify top customers for special offers
2. **Monitor Visit Frequency** - Note customers who haven't visited recently
3. **Personalize Service** - Review customer notes before they arrive
4. **Recognize Milestones** - Celebrate spending milestones or anniversaries

## Data Integration

### Customer Table Schema

- `id` - Unique customer identifier
- `full_name` - Customer's name
- `phone` - Contact phone number
- `email` - Email address
- `notes` - Free-text notes field
- `created_at` - Account creation timestamp

### Sales Relationship

Customers are linked to sales via `customer_id` foreign key. The system:

1. Fetches all customers (paginated)
2. Retrieves all sales for those customers filtered by active branch
3. Aggregates totals, payments, and dues
4. Calculates last sale date
5. Joins data for display

<Tip>
The system automatically handles customers with no sales history, displaying zeros and "Never" for last visit.
</Tip>

## Common Workflows

### Adding a New Customer

1. Click add customer button in top right
2. Enter full name (required)
3. Enter phone number
4. Add any notes about preferences
5. Click save
6. Customer is now available for POS selection

### Finding a Customer at POS

1. During sale creation, use customer search
2. Type partial name or phone number
3. Select matching customer from results
4. Customer details auto-populate in sale
5. Previous transaction history is accessible

### Collecting Outstanding Dues

1. Navigate to Customers page
2. Review Page Outstanding metric
3. Sort table by Outstanding column (highest first)
4. Click on customer with dues
5. View sale history
6. Record payment against specific sale
7. Outstanding automatically recalculates

### Generating Customer Insights

1. Sort by Total Spend to find VIP customers
2. Sort by Last Visit to find inactive customers
3. Export top 12 customers list for marketing
4. Plan re-engagement campaigns for dormant customers
5. Offer loyalty rewards to high spenders

## Multi-Branch Considerations

While customer profiles are organization-wide:

- **Sales data** is filtered by your active branch
- **Total spend** only includes purchases from your branch
- **Outstanding** only shows dues from your branch sales
- **Last visit** is the most recent sale at your branch

If a customer visits multiple branches:

- Profile is shared and updated across branches
- Each branch sees only their own sales data
- Organization owner can view consolidated data

<Note>
This design allows customers to shop at any location while maintaining data privacy between branches.
</Note>
