The Vendor & Purchases system tracks your supplier relationships, procurement history, payment obligations, and outstanding balances across your supply chain.

## Overview

Vendor management provides comprehensive tracking of suppliers, purchase orders, received inventory, and payment status. All purchase data is filtered by your active branch while vendor profiles remain organization-wide.

<Note>
You must be assigned to a branch before managing vendor records. Without branch assignment, you'll see "No branch assigned" with instructions to contact your administrator.
</Note>

## Search Functionality

The search feature supports finding vendors by:

- **Vendor name** (case-insensitive partial match)
- **Phone number** (case-insensitive partial match)
- **Email address** (case-insensitive partial match)

Search uses SQL ILIKE with wildcard matching for flexible queries.

<Tip>
You can search by any part of the vendor name, phone, or email - the system automatically matches partial strings.
</Tip>

## Outstanding Balances Table

The primary table displays vendors sorted by outstanding balance (highest first), showing up to 10 vendors:

### Columns

- **Vendor** - Supplier name in medium font weight
- **Phone** - Contact phone number, or "—" if not provided
- **Orders** - Total number of purchase orders from this vendor
- **Procured** - Total purchase amount in Indian Rupees (₹)
- **Outstanding** - Unpaid balance, or "—" if fully paid
- **Last Purchase** - Date of most recent purchase in "DD MMM YYYY" format, or "Never"
- **Actions** - Dropdown menu for vendor operations

### Empty State

When no vendor purchases exist:

> "No vendor purchases recorded yet. Log a purchase to see standings."

## Vendor Analytics

For each vendor, the system automatically calculates:

### Procurement Metrics

- **Total Procured** - Sum of all `total_amount` from purchases
- **Orders** - Count of purchase orders
- **Last Purchase** - Most recent `purchase_date`

### Payment Tracking

- **Total Paid** - Sum of all payments made to vendor
- **Last Payment** - Most recent `paid_at` timestamp
- **Outstanding** - Calculated as `total_procured - total_paid`

<Note>
Outstanding balance calculation: `max(total_procured - total_paid, 0)` ensures non-negative values.
</Note>

## Best Practices

### Vendor Onboarding

1. **Complete Profile Information** - Capture GST number, contact details, and address during onboarding
2. **Verify GST Numbers** - Validate GST numbers against government database
3. **Document Terms** - Note payment terms and credit limits in vendor notes
4. **Emergency Contacts** - Store alternate phone numbers and email addresses

### Purchase Management

1. **Consistent Recording** - Log every purchase immediately upon receipt
2. **Link to Branch** - Ensure purchases are assigned to the correct branch
3. **Verify Amounts** - Double-check total_amount against vendor invoice
4. **Track Delivery Dates** - Use purchase_date for when goods were received

### Payment Discipline

1. **Prioritize by Outstanding** - The Outstanding Balances table sorts automatically
2. **Pay on Schedule** - Maintain good supplier relationships with timely payments
3. **Record Partial Payments** - Log every payment, even if partial
4. **Reconcile Monthly** - Compare your records with vendor statements

### Supplier Relationships

1. **Monitor Top Suppliers** - Identify your most important vendors by procured amount
2. **Diversify Sources** - Don't over-rely on single suppliers
3. **Track Performance** - Note quality issues or delivery delays
4. **Negotiate Terms** - Use purchase history data in price negotiations

## Data Integration

### Vendor Table Schema

- `id` - Unique vendor identifier
- `name` - Supplier business name
- `contact_name` - Contact person
- `phone` - Contact phone number
- `email` - Contact email address
- `gst_number` - Tax registration number
- `address` - Physical address
- `created_at` - Record creation timestamp

### Purchase Relationship

Vendors link to purchases via `vendor_id`. The system:

1. Fetches paginated vendor list
2. Retrieves all purchases for those vendors filtered by active branch
3. Fetches all payments linked to those purchases
4. Aggregates totals, orders, and dates
5. Calculates outstanding balances
6. Sorts and displays results

### Payment Relationship

Payments link to purchases via `purchase_id` and vendors via `vendor_id`:

- Payments without purchase_id are general vendor payments
- Payments with purchase_id are specific to that purchase
- System filters payments to only include those for relevant purchases

<Tip>
The system only counts payments associated with purchases from your active branch, preventing cross-branch data leakage.
</Tip>

## Common Workflows

### Adding a New Vendor

1. Click add vendor button in top right
2. Enter vendor name (required)
3. Add contact person and details
4. Enter GST number if available
5. Add address for delivery coordination
6. Click save
7. Vendor is now available for purchase creation

### Recording a Purchase

1. From vendor actions menu, select "Record new purchase"
2. Or navigate to `/purchases/new`
3. Select vendor from dropdown
4. Enter purchase date (when goods received)
5. Add line items with quantities and costs
6. System calculates total_amount
7. Save purchase record
8. Outstanding balance updates automatically

### Making a Payment

1. Navigate to vendor with outstanding balance
2. Click actions menu > "Log payment"
3. Select which purchase(s) to pay against
4. Enter payment amount
5. Set payment date (paid_at)
6. Note payment method if needed
7. Save payment
8. Outstanding recalculates instantly

### Reconciling Vendor Statements

1. Request statement from vendor
2. Filter your purchases by vendor
3. Compare vendor statement line items with your purchase records
4. Identify any discrepancies
5. Log any missing payments
6. Contact vendor about any unexplained charges

### Managing Overdue Payables

1. Sort Outstanding Balances table by highest first
2. Review last purchase date for each
3. Calculate payment terms elapsed
4. Prioritize vendors with oldest outstanding balances
5. Arrange payment or communicate revised schedule
6. Log payments as made

## Multi-Branch Considerations

Vendor profiles are organization-wide, but:

- **Purchase records** are branch-specific
- **Procured amounts** only include your branch purchases
- **Outstanding balances** only reflect your branch payables
- **Payment records** are filtered to your branch purchases

If multiple branches buy from the same vendor:

- Vendor profile is shared across branches
- Each branch sees only their own purchases and payments
- Organization owner can view consolidated vendor data
- GST number and contact details are centrally managed

<Note>
This design maintains data privacy between branches while avoiding duplicate vendor profiles.
</Note>

## Reporting and Analysis

Use vendor data for:

- **Cash flow planning** - Outstanding balances inform payment schedules
- **Supplier concentration** - Procured amounts show dependency levels
- **Inventory planning** - Orders count and last purchase date guide reordering
- **Cost analysis** - Track price trends across purchase history
- **Tax compliance** - GST numbers and amounts for reporting
