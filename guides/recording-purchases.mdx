This guide walks you through recording inventory purchases from vendors, tracking payments, and managing your supplier relationships.

## Understanding Purchases

The purchase system helps you track:

- **Vendors**: Suppliers you buy inventory from
- **Purchase orders**: Records of inventory received from vendors
- **Purchase items**: Individual items and quantities in each order
- **Vendor payments**: Payments made to suppliers over time

## Prerequisites

Before recording purchases:

- You must be assigned to a branch (managers and owners)
- Items and variants should exist in your inventory
- Vendors should be created (or create them during the purchase flow)

## Managing Vendors

Vendors are the suppliers you purchase inventory from.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Vendor">
    Before recording your first purchase, add the vendor:
    
    Navigate to the vendors section and click **"Add vendor"**.
    
    Required information:
    - **Name**: Vendor's business name (must be unique)
    - **Contact name**: Primary contact person
    - **Phone**: Contact phone number
    - **Email**: Vendor's email address
    - **GST number**: Tax identification (for Indian businesses)
    - **Address**: Vendor's business address
    
    Only the name is required, but complete information helps with tax compliance and communication.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Search Vendors">
    Use the search functionality to find vendors by:
    
    - Business name
    - Contact phone
    - Email address
    
    This is helpful when you have many suppliers.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Edit Vendor Details">
    Update vendor information as needed:
    
    - Change contact details
    - Update GST number
    - Modify address
    
    Changes don't affect historical purchase records.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recording a Purchase Order

When you receive inventory from a vendor, create a purchase order to track it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Purchases">
    From the main navigation, go to **Purchases**.
    
    If you're not assigned to a branch, you'll see a message to get branch access first.
    
    The dashboard displays:
    - **Orders**: Total purchase orders for your branch
    - **Page value**: Total value of visible purchase orders
    - **Paid**: Amount paid to vendors
    - **Due**: Outstanding amounts owed to vendors
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start a New Purchase">
    Click **"Add purchase"** in the top-right corner.
    
    You'll be prompted to select:
    - **Vendor**: Choose from existing vendors or create a new one
    - **Invoice number**: Vendor's invoice/bill number (optional but recommended)
    - **Purchase date**: When you received the items (defaults to today)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Purchase Items">
    For each item in the shipment:
    
    1. Select the **item variant** from your inventory
    2. Enter the **quantity** received
    3. Enter the **unit cost** (what the vendor charged per unit)
    4. Apply **discount** if the vendor gave a line-item discount
    
    The system automatically calculates:
    - Total cost per line item
    - Subtotal for all items
  </Step>

  <Step title="Apply Discounts and Taxes">
    At the purchase level, you can add:
    
    - **Overall discount**: Discount applied to the entire order
    - **Tax total**: GST or other applicable taxes
    
    The system calculates:
    ```
    Subtotal = Sum of all line items
    Discount total = Line discounts + overall discount
    Tax total = Applicable taxes
    Total amount = Subtotal - Discount total + Tax total
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Notes">
    Use the notes field for:
    
    - Quality observations
    - Shipment condition
    - Special terms from the vendor
    - Reference numbers
    
    Notes help track issues or special conditions for future reference.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the Purchase">
    Click **"Record purchase"** to save the order.
    
    The purchase appears in the recent purchases table showing:
    - Invoice number (or auto-generated ID)
    - Vendor name
    - Total quantity of items
    - Total amount
    - Paid amount (initially ₹0)
    - Due amount (equals total amount initially)
    - Purchase date
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recording Vendor Payments

Track payments to vendors to manage outstanding balances and cash flow.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Payment Dialog">
    From the purchases page, find the purchase order and click **"Record payment"**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Payment Details">
    Fill in the payment information:
    
    - **Amount**: How much you're paying (can be partial or full)
    - **Payment method**: Select from:
      - Cash
      - UPI
      - Card
      - Other
    - **Reference**: Transaction ID, check number, or UPI reference
    - **Notes**: Additional context about the payment
    - **Paid at**: Date and time of payment (defaults to now)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review Outstanding Balance">
    After recording payment, the system updates:
    
    - **Paid amount**: Total paid so far
    - **Due amount**: Remaining balance
    
    The purchase status shows:
    - "Due ₹X" if any balance remains
    - "Paid ₹X" if fully paid
  </Step>

  <Step title="Record Multiple Payments">
    For large purchases, you can make multiple payments:
    
    1. Record the first payment (e.g., advance payment)
    2. Record subsequent payments as you make them
    3. Each payment is tracked with its own method, date, and reference
    
    This gives you complete payment history per purchase order.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Viewing Purchase Analytics

The purchases dashboard provides insights into your vendor spending:

### Recent Purchases Table

Shows the latest purchase orders with:
- Invoice number or generated ID
- Vendor name
- Total quantity of units received
- Total amount of the purchase
- Amount paid to date
- Outstanding due amount
- Purchase date

### Latest Payments Section

Displays recent vendor payments:
- Purchase reference
- Payment amount
- Payment date

Use this to track cash outflows and verify payment posting.

### Search and Filter

Search purchases by:
- Invoice number
- Vendor name
- Notes content

Results are paginated with 10 purchases per page.

## Best Practices

### Invoice Numbering

Always enter the vendor's invoice number:
- Makes it easier to reconcile with vendor statements
- Helps during tax filing
- Simplifies dispute resolution
- Prevents duplicate entry of the same shipment

### Payment Tracking

```
Do:
✓ Record payments immediately after making them
✓ Include UPI/check reference numbers
✓ Note payment terms in purchase notes
✓ Review outstanding dues regularly

Don't:
✗ Wait to record payments in batches
✗ Skip payment references
✗ Forget to verify paid amounts
```

### Quality Control

Use the notes field during purchase to document:
- Item condition on arrival
- Any damaged or defective pieces
- Missing items vs. invoice
- Agreement on returns or credits

This creates a record if you need to claim credit or return items later.

<Accordion title="Can I edit a purchase after saving it?">
  Yes, managers and owners can edit purchase orders to correct mistakes:
  
  - Update quantities or costs
  - Change invoice number
  - Modify taxes or discounts
  - Add or remove items
  
  However, best practice is to enter accurately the first time and use notes to document any post-delivery adjustments.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if I pay the vendor before creating the purchase order?">
  Two approaches:
  
  1. **Recommended**: Create the purchase first, then record the payment
  2. **Alternative**: Create the purchase with the payment date backdated
  
  The system tracks when payments occurred, not when they were entered, so backdating maintains accurate financial records.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I handle vendor returns or credits?">
  For items you return to vendors:
  
  1. Add notes to the original purchase documenting the return
  2. If the vendor issues a credit memo, record a negative payment or create a new purchase with negative quantity
  3. Update the notes with credit terms and resolution
  
  Work with your accountant to determine the best approach for your business and tax compliance needs.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I see total spending per vendor?">
  While the current view shows per-purchase details, you can:
  
  - Search for a specific vendor to see all their purchases
  - Review the payment history for each purchase
  - Export data for detailed vendor spending analysis
  
  Vendor analytics features may be added in future updates.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do purchases automatically update stock levels?">
  This depends on your system configuration:
  
  - If stock tracking is enabled, purchases increase stock levels automatically
  - If stock tracking is not enabled, purchases are recorded for financial purposes but don't affect inventory counts
  
  Check with your system administrator or owner to understand your setup.
</Accordion>
